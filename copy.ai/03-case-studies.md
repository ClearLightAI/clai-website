## Case Study 1: Spartans Gym & Fitness
**Client type:** Multi-site fitness and wellness business
**Location:** Australia
**Engagement:** AI Readiness Audit — CEO + 5 department managers

---

### The Situation

Danny Vorhauer is CEO of a multi-entity conglomerate including Spartans Gym & Fitness (35,000 sq ft facility), The Zone (reformer pilates studio), and several other businesses. He had 30 years of leadership experience and was working 15-18 hour days, 7 days a week, with no days off in over 32 days at the time of the audit.

His role had evolved into a high-intensity "Master Problem Solver" - the primary bottleneck for over 50 managers, handling 200+ daily communications across email, Slack, and Discord. He was spending 3-5 hours per week on genuine strategic work. The rest was firefighting.

His team of managers were similarly burdened - each losing 20-50% of their week to manual tasks, internal information requests, and repetitive administrative loops.

---

### What We Did

We conducted AI Readiness Interviews with six people across the organisation:
- Danny Vorhauer (CEO)
- Sharry (General Manager)
- Shiela David (Membercare Manager)
- Irene (Reception Manager)
- Tahlia (Reformer Pilates Manager)
- Gab (The Zone Manager)

Each interview covered: daily tasks, biggest time drains, frustration levels, previous automation attempts, tools used, and what "good" would look like.

We then produced individual AI Readiness Reports for each team member, plus a consolidated master report with phased implementation recommendations.

---

### Key Findings

**Danny (CEO)**
- Spending 4-6 hours/day on email and message triage (200+ messages daily)
- Waking at 3:30 AM daily to physically walk through the gym to check cleaning standards
- Primary bottleneck for all 50+ managers
- High-value strategic work: 3-5 hours per week

Recommendations:
- AI Chief of Staff agent (LLM integrated with Gmail/Slack) to draft responses and triage communications - estimated 3-4 hours/day saved
- AI-verified maintenance system (photo-based checklist app with Vision AI analysis) to eliminate 3:30 AM audits - 1.5 hours/day saved
- Internal manager knowledge bot trained on policies and Pulse reports
- Automated CEO Weekly Pulse synthesiser pulling from Monday.com and Slack

**Shiela (Membercare)**
- Spending half a day per week manually filtering debt reports and sending individual payment-chasing emails
- Managing a manual cancellation process requiring cross-referencing across multiple systems
- Estimated 20-30% of workweek recoverable through automation

**Irene (Reception)**
- Losing up to 30% of her week to "information detective work" - acting as the human bridge between disparate systems
- Manual inventory management and maintenance coordination
- Estimated 1.5 workdays per week recoverable

**Gab (The Zone Manager)**
- Only 33% of her time (5 hours/week) spent on high-value work
- Acting as the operational "Wiki" for the entire gym via Slack
- 10 years of institutional knowledge trapped in a personal OneNote file
- Primary recommendation: AI-powered Slack bot built from "The Bible" - her OneNote knowledge base

**Tahlia (Reformer Pilates)**
- Losing 5-6 hours/week to manual data extraction, invoice cross-referencing, and content creation
- Managing the full studio lifecycle solo - strategy to physical maintenance
- Primary recommendation: AI-automated reporting and social media content engine

---

### Phase 1 Recommendation

**Manager Support Agent** - an AI knowledge base and internal support system that answers common manager questions without requiring Danny's input. Estimated to significantly reduce inbound communication volume and free Danny for strategic work.

---

### Result

The Spartans audit demonstrated that AI can reclaim the equivalent of multiple full-time employees worth of productive hours per week - without adding headcount. The total recoverable time across the six-person team audited exceeded 25+ hours per week.

The Phase 1 build brief was delivered. Implementation began in March 2026.

## Case Study 2: Human Garage / Centred
**Client type:** Certification program for coaches and bodyworkers
**Engagement:** AI Lead Scoring System — full dataset analysis and live implementation

---

### The Situation

Human Garage (operating through their sales arm, Centred) runs a high-ticket certification program for coaches, healers, and bodywork practitioners. They had hundreds of inbound applications per month and a sales team manually reviewing each one before making contact.

The problem: not all leads are equal. Some applicants are highly qualified buyers. Others are aspirational but financially unready. The sales team had no fast way to tell them apart.

---

### What We Did

We built an AI lead scoring system trained on 472 historical contacts with known outcomes (won or lost). We analysed application responses, demographic patterns, and financial indicators to identify what actually predicts conversion.

The system was then deployed live into their GoHighLevel CRM. Within approximately 20 seconds of any new form submission, the system:
1. Scores the lead on a 0-40 scale across four components
2. Assigns a High / Medium / Low tier label
3. Writes a 2-3 sentence lead summary for the sales rep
4. Suggests a personalised opening line for the first call
5. Shows a numeric breakdown of where the lead is strong or weak

---

### Key Findings from the Data

**Financial readiness is the #1 conversion predictor - by a large margin.**
- Applicants with funds available or good credit converted at 53%
- Applicants with tight or no funding converted at 2%
- That is a 26x difference

**Urgency is not a reliable signal.**
- "ASAP" and "in the next 3-6 months" converted at the same rate (26%)
- Zero of 54 applicants who said "ASAP" but had tight/no funding ever converted
- Not one

**Application quality (length and specificity) predicts commitment.**
- Winners wrote 58 words on average in their application answers
- Losers wrote 29
- Winners used language tied to professional application ("nervous system," "certification," "Human Garage")
- Losers used more generic language ("enhance," "wellbeing," "purpose")

**Score thresholds validated on 472 contacts:**
- Score 26+: 52% historical conversion rate
- Score 15-25: 14% historical conversion rate
- Score below 15: 0% conversion rate - zero buyers in the entire dataset scored this low

**No winner ever scored Low. The system has never missed a real buyer.**

---

### V2 Expansion

The analysis was later expanded to 760 contacts (V2), confirming all core V1 findings and adding:
- Geographic conversion rate analysis
- Funding vs. timing cross-tab
- Deeper language pattern analysis tied to website copy
- Revised pipeline revenue estimates

---

### The Cost

Approximately $0.01 per lead scored. At typical application volumes for Human Garage: $2-6 per month to operate.

---

### Why This Matters as a Case Study

This engagement shows what ClearLight AI produces beyond the basic audit:
- Data-driven systems, not opinions
- Findings that change how the sales team operates every day
- Infrastructure that runs continuously at near-zero cost
- Results that compound over time as the dataset grows

## Case Study 3: Reid Perkins / 9D Breathwork
**Client type:** Online wellness and breathwork certification platform
**Engagement:** Company Brain (RAG knowledge agent) + Company Pulse CEO assistant

---

### Overview

Reid Perkins operates 9D Breathwork, a global online breathwork platform. The engagement involved building two connected AI systems:

**Company Brain** - a RAG-based AI knowledge agent deployed in Slack. Staff could ask questions and receive accurate answers sourced from the company's own documents, SOPs, and knowledge base.

**Company Pulse** - a custom CEO assistant designed to synthesise operational updates, flag priorities, and help Reid maintain oversight without being buried in individual messages.

Both systems were integrated directly into the team's existing Slack workspace.

## Case Study 6: Graeme Taylor / The Online Dog Trainer
**Client type:** Online education and dog training platform
**Engagement:** Private AI deployment (OpenWebUI on AWS)

---

### Overview

Graeme Taylor is the founder of The Online Dog Trainer, one of the world's largest online dog training platforms. The engagement involved deploying a private, self-hosted AI platform on AWS infrastructure using Docker and OpenRouter as the model provider.

The deployment provides Graeme and his team with a custom ChatGPT-style interface with:
